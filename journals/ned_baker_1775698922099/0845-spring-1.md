# Ned the Baker

**Spring, Day 1, Year 845**

Someone has been in your workroom. You can tell by the way the flour sacks are placed, by the smell of a stranger in a space that is all yours. Nothing is missing but you notice the book on the high shelf has been moved. The book with your grandmother's recipe inside it, the one you have never shared, the one that makes your work unmistakably yours. You know from the footprint near the window that it was someone who has been in kitchens, someone who knew what they were looking for.

> Check whether the recipe page has been copied

The recipe page has been handled, you can see a faint impression from pressing fingers at the corner. Whether it was memorized or copied you cannot be certain. The window latch has been worked from outside, recently. You ask at the baker's stall two streets over and the woman there remembers seeing Rosie pass your lane yesterday evening, lingering by the side alley. She offers the information with the cheerful neutrality of someone who enjoys the drama but does not want to be named.

> Take this to the guild and let them arbitrate

Whatever the outcome, the recipe is still yours. The knowledge of how to make the thing your grandmother made, with the particular adjustment she discovered late in her life and never wrote down anywhere but that one book, lives in your hands now as much as on that page. You think about tradition, about what gets passed on and what gets protected. There is a difference between holding something close and hoarding it. You are still working out where that line sits.

> Write the full recipe out in a guild ledger so it is formally yours on record

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.
