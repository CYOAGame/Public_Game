# Drift the Wanderer

**Summer, Day 5, Year 866**

You find it deep in the Ashwood an oak that should not be possible. Three men could not link hands around its trunk. The bark is carved in patterns you half-recognise: the same shapes from the shrine in the cellar. The same shapes from the passage below the square. The roots have pushed up the ground in a ring and the air smells of stone and iron and something very old. Lydia is crouched at the base, copying the carvings into a small book.

> Study the carvings carefully alongside Lydia

Lydia shows you their notes. The carvings are a map not of roads, but of something beneath them. Confluences. Nodes. The tree sits at the centre of a web that includes the cellar, the passage, the old mill, and a point outside town you cannot identify. "This was not made by human hands," Lydia says quietly. "Or not only human." They look at the tree and back at you. "What do we do with this?"

> Agree to keep this between the two of you for now

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.
