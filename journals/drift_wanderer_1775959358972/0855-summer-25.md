# Drift the Wanderer

**Summer, Day 25, Year 855**

You tracked the wolves into the hills. The trail is fresh and the den is closer than expected.

The tracks lead you to a slope in the Ashwood, a rocky hollow half-hidden by fallen timber. You find the den at dusk and the den is not empty. A grey she-wolf lies inside, three cubs pressed against her flank. She does not move toward you. She watches you with yellow eyes, perfectly still. Brenna is behind you, bow raised. "Say the word," they say quietly.

> Back off and think, there may be a way to move them without killing

You and Brenna spend the next day returning with the carcass of a deer killed elsewhere, leaving a trail further into the deep Ashwood, away from the farmsteads. It takes two attempts. On the third day the den is empty and the family has moved north, deeper into the Ashwood and toward the clearing with the stone. The sheep are safe. The wolves are alive. And you have, in effect, brought them closer to whatever drew them toward town in the first place.

> Keep it between you and Brenna, no point explaining

Back in Ironhaven, you give what account seems right. The sheep are safer than they were this morning. Whether the wolves are gone, relocated, or merely moved is a question for the next few weeks. {shepherd.name} is grateful. Brenna is professional about it. The Ashwood is still out there, and you know more about what is in it than you did before. That knowledge is either an advantage or a weight, depending on what comes next.

> Keep the clearing to yourself, report only what the town needs to know

A week after the den, the flocks are safe. The Ashwood is quiet in its ordinary way, not the charged quiet you noticed near the clearing. Life in Ironhaven returns to its rhythms. But the wolves are still out there, the fourth wolf is still out there, and the stone in the clearing still hums with something below it. None of that has been resolved. It has been set aside.

> Make a firm decision to return to the clearing with proper preparation

Preparing for the clearing requires thinking through what you actually expect to find. The stone is warm. The wolf tends it. Something below the town may be connected to it. You do not know what you are walking into. Brenna can come with you, or you can go alone. You have three days before the next market when the roads are busy enough to cover your movement.

> Bring Brenna. Two sets of eyes at the clearing is better than one.

Whether you wait at the stone or examine it, the conclusion is the same: the large wolf has been tending this place. Coming here regularly. Pressing against the stone. The wolves were not driven out of the Ashwood by fear alone. They were called toward the town because something below the town is pulling at things that can sense it. The binding under the cellar is like a fire, and the wolves have been drawn to its warmth for months. The fourth wolf is its keeper. Whether that is its own instinct or something the binding shaped, you cannot say.

> This goes to Brenna and anyone who knows about the binding

Back in Ironhaven, you give what account seems right. The sheep are safer than they were this morning. Whether the wolves are gone, relocated, or merely moved is a question for the next few weeks. {shepherd.name} is grateful. Brenna is professional about it. The Ashwood is still out there, and you know more about what is in it than you did before. That knowledge is either an advantage or a weight, depending on what comes next.

> Ask {shepherd.name} to send word if they see the wolves again

A week after the den, the flocks are safe. The Ashwood is quiet in its ordinary way, not the charged quiet you noticed near the clearing. Life in Ironhaven returns to its rhythms. But the wolves are still out there, the fourth wolf is still out there, and the stone in the clearing still hums with something below it. None of that has been resolved. It has been set aside.

> Let it rest. The town is safe enough for now.

A week after leaving the den, the Ashwood returns to its ordinary silence. The flocks have settled. Brenna makes one more trip to check the north part of the wood and comes back with nothing urgent to report. "The stone is still there," they say. "Still warm." Neither of you says what that means. Some things need more time before they can be named.

> Leave it for now. The town is safe and the mystery can wait.

A month after the den discovery. The hunter has been quieter than usual about it, which could mean nothing. At the market, you overhear someone saying the wolf that spooked the shepherd was never from the Ashwood, it came down from the northern hills. That account does not match what you found. Someone's story has drifted, or someone needs it to. You know what you saw.

> Let the story drift. It keeps people out of the Ashwood without them having to be warned.

The day draws to a close.
