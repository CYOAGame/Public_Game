# Drift the Wanderer

**Summer, Day 12, Year 816**

The market square hums with the ordinary noise of a market morning. Bread smells. Argument over change. A child weaving between legs. You have no particular errand just time to spend and a bit of coin in your pocket. The stalls stretch in two long rows toward the well.

> Head straight to Lydia's stall you know the stock there

Lydia's stall is well-stocked as always. But something is off. They are watching the square instead of their customers, and when you pick up a sack of grain and ask the price, they name it without hesitation twelve copper then look away before you can react. No haggling offered. No charm. As if the number were already decided for them.

> Ask Lydia why they are not haggling today that is not like them

Lydia straightens when you raise the prices. "Supply and demand," they say too quickly, too flatly. "Grain is short this season. Everyone knows it." You point out that four stalls all arrived at the same number independently. A muscle in their jaw tightens. "I set my prices. That is my right." They start rearranging a stack of sacks that did not need rearranging.

> Push harder "That is not an explanation, that is a deflection"

Lydia leans close, voice dropping to something between a warning and a plea. "You are asking questions about things that are not your business. I would strongly suggest you spend your coin and move on." Then they step back, composure restored, and give you a merchant's smile. It does not reach their eyes. Someone is watching from a stall across the square you notice before they look away.

> Watch Lydia wait to see if they signal whoever was watching

You keep your distance and watch. Mid-morning, Lydia hands the stall to a boy and slips into the alley that runs behind the tanner's shop. You follow at a gap. The alley smells of hide and old water. At the far end, Lydia stops and waits, arms folded. Someone else arrives from the other direction a figure with a hood up despite the mild weather.

> Step out. Make your presence known.

You step forward. The hooded figure sees you and bolts fast, practiced, gone around the corner before you can move. Lydia turns slowly. Their expression is not fear. It is something colder. "You have no idea what you have done," they say, voice very quiet. "That person keeps things orderly. Without them, the people who are actually running this will send someone less subtle."

> Tell me who they are. Tell me who is running this.

You know enough to know something is wrong. The question is where to pull. Lydia is still at their stall, watching the square with guarded eyes. The guild hall is a short walk. Brenna passes the market square on their patrol route you have spoken to them before. And there is a large warehouse near the east gate that services most of the market's bulk goods.
