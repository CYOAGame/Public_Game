# Drift the Wanderer

**Spring, Day 19, Year 845**

A stranger slips through the town gate just before dusk, dusty, limping, clutching a worn leather satchel to their chest. Ned scans the square with wide, frightened eyes and fixes on you. Not on the nearest face. On yours specifically. "Please," they rasp. "I need somewhere safe. Just for tonight." Behind them, Kira is watching from the gatehouse. The gate will close in minutes.

> Pull them inside and find a hiding place

You find Ned crouched behind old barrel staves in the cooperage, pressing the satchel against their body with both arms. "You have no idea what you have done for me," they whisper. "Or what is in here." They look exhausted down to the bone, but their eyes are sharp and calculating. This is someone who thinks even when they are afraid. After a moment: "Do you want to know? I will tell you, if you want. You have earned the right to know what you are protecting."

> Ask what they are carrying

Ned speaks carefully, choosing each word. The satchel contains documents that could ruin a name in this town. They copied them from a ledger they had legitimate access to. "Account records," they say. "A diversion of funds. Three towns, including Ironhaven. The name on the account has standing here." They stop. "I am not a thief. I am a clerk who made a copy and ran when the wrong person saw me do it. The men sent after me are a day behind, at most." The words land quietly and settle heavily.

> Ask whose name is on the account

Ned tells you the name. You recognize it. Of course you do. It is the kind of name that appears on dedication plaques and civic records, attached to generous donations and public goodwill. The name of someone who has been building a reputation for years, carefully, on a foundation that is apparently stolen. You sit with that for a moment.

> Say you believe them and you will help

"I need the documents to reach someone who cannot be bought," Ned says. "The miller's widow on the east road. She was a magistrate's clerk twenty years ago. She knows what to do with evidence." They press a small sealed letter into your hand. "That is for her. The satchel stays with me until I know the way is clear. But I need someone who knows this town." They look at you steadily. "Can you get word to her?"

> Agree to carry word to the miller's widow

By mid-morning, two riders are in the square. Well-dressed, unhurried, asking quiet questions of stall-holders. Not guards. Not merchants. Their horses are good and their hands say they know how to use what is at their belts. Someone has already pointed in your direction. You can see it in the way one of them scans the square and pauses when their eyes find you. Ned is not in sight. You have perhaps a minute before one of them crosses the square.

> Walk directly to the riders and introduce yourself, own the moment

You walk to them directly and introduce yourself by name. You ask, pleasantly, what brings them to Ironhaven. The lead rider measures you, looking for the tell of someone hiding something. You give them nothing. After a careful exchange they admit they are looking for a person, a traveler with a leather bag. You say you saw someone matching that description heading east before dawn. They thank you, coldly, and ride. You have bought time. Possibly a day.

> Go to Kira now and tell them what just happened

The riders leave Ironhaven at dusk, with or without what they came for, depending on what you did. Ned is somewhere: gone to safety, in custody, in hiding, or sitting across from a very uncomfortable town official with a sheaf of ledger copies on the table between them. The documents are somewhere too. The name attached to the fraud is known to you. Whether it becomes known to anyone else is, largely, your decision. Ironhaven will feel the shape of this evening for a while.
