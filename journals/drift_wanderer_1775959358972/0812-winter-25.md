# Drift the Wanderer

**Winter, Day 25, Year 812**

You tracked the wolves into the hills. The trail is fresh and the den is closer than expected.

The tracks lead you to a slope in the Ashwood, a rocky hollow half-hidden by fallen timber. You find the den at dusk and the den is not empty. A grey she-wolf lies inside, three cubs pressed against her flank. She does not move toward you. She watches you with yellow eyes, perfectly still. Voss is behind you, bow raised. "Say the word," they say quietly.

> Give the word, wolves near town are a threat, cubs or not

Voss makes it quick. You drag the animals clear of the den and mark the hollow. On the walk back, neither of you says much. The cubs were small. The threat to the flocks is gone. Voss says: "You made the right call." They mean it. You are not entirely sure. That night you find yourself thinking about the clearing and the warm stone, and what used to tend it.

> Accept it. Hard things sometimes need doing.

Three days later, Voss comes to find you. They have been in the Ashwood. "The clearing," they say. "The stone. It is cold now. It was warm last week. I could have sworn." They look uncomfortable. "I think the wolves were doing something up there. I do not know what. But something was. And we stopped it." They are not asking for absolution. They are asking if you noticed the same thing.

> Confirm it. The stone was connected to the wolves somehow. Unclear how.

Back in Ironhaven, you give what account seems right. The sheep are safer than they were this morning. Whether the wolves are gone, relocated, or merely moved is a question for the next few weeks. {shepherd.name} is grateful. Voss is professional about it. The Ashwood is still out there, and you know more about what is in it than you did before. That knowledge is either an advantage or a weight, depending on what comes next.

> Ask {shepherd.name} to send word if they see the wolves again

A week after the den, the flocks are safe. The Ashwood is quiet in its ordinary way, not the charged quiet you noticed near the clearing. Life in Ironhaven returns to its rhythms. But the wolves are still out there, the fourth wolf is still out there, and the stone in the clearing still hums with something below it. None of that has been resolved. It has been set aside.

> Make a firm decision to return to the clearing with proper preparation

Preparing for the clearing requires thinking through what you actually expect to find. The stone is warm. The wolf tends it. Something below the town may be connected to it. You do not know what you are walking into. Voss can come with you, or you can go alone. You have three days before the next market when the roads are busy enough to cover your movement.

> Go alone. What you find there may be something Voss should not see yet.

Whether you wait at the stone or examine it, the conclusion is the same: the large wolf has been tending this place. Coming here regularly. Pressing against the stone. The wolves were not driven out of the Ashwood by fear alone. They were called toward the town because something below the town is pulling at things that can sense it. The binding under the cellar is like a fire, and the wolves have been drawn to its warmth for months. The fourth wolf is its keeper. Whether that is its own instinct or something the binding shaped, you cannot say.

> Hold this to yourself for now. The implications are too large to speak aloud yet.
