# Drift the Wanderer

**Autumn, Day 17, Year 821**

The road is wet and the weight of your pack has found every sore point on your shoulders. You have been walking since before the town's bells, and the town is far enough behind you now that you can stop calculating whether anyone recognized your face. The margin is not yet comfortable. It may never be comfortable. You come over a rise and see a farmhouse with smoke in the chimney, and Brenna in the yard, who looks up and sees you and does not look away, which is already more than most people do.

> Walk up to the house and ask if there is work or shelter to trade for

Brenna offers a barn, a meal, no questions. The no-questions part is said deliberately, which means they have looked at you and drawn conclusions, and have decided the conclusions are their business and not yours to confess to. The barn is dry and the meal is simple and adequate. You sit across from them and eat and try to remember the last time someone did something for you without a ledger behind it. The memory is further back than you expected.

> Accept simply and gratefully. Say thank you and mean it.

Morning. Brenna is already up, moving around the yard with the efficiency of someone whose life runs on its own schedule without consulting anyone. They do not ask where you are going. They hand you something for the road: food, a length of rope, a direction. "The Millfield track is clear," they say. "Avoid the east checkpoint for the next few days." They say it to their fence post, not to you, in the tone of someone noting the weather. You understand. You shoulder your pack.

> Ask their name before you go

The road opens ahead and you walk into it. Whatever drove you out here, and there are mornings when the specifics have worn smooth with repetition, the road itself is neutral. It does not judge. The weight on your shoulders is the same weight as yesterday, but something is slightly different in how you are carrying it. There are people who will offer a barn and ask nothing. You know one of them now.

> Take the Millfield track as advised and avoid the checkpoint

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.
