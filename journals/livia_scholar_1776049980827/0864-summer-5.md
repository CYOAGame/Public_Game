# Livia the Scholar

**Summer, Day 5, Year 864**

Three things in one morning: a crow lands on your windowsill and will not leave. The milk Garrick left out has curdled solid, in summer. And at noon high summer noon a dog howls from three streets over and is answered by two more. People stop in the street. Oswin draws a ward-sign in the dust with their boot. "That is the third sign this week," they say to nobody in particular.

> Say nothing. You feel it too something is wrong.

You find yourself checking the windows twice that evening. The feeling does not have a name not quite dread, not quite warning but it sits in your chest like a stone. Garrick stops by after dark. "You felt it too," they say. It is not a question.

> Admit it yes, you felt it too

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.

> Take it seriously ask the elder what the signs mean

Oswin lists the signs on their fingers: crow at the threshold means a death is near. Curdled milk unprovoked means the land is sickening. Dogs at noon means something is approaching that the living cannot see. "What does it add up to?" you ask. The elder looks at the horizon. "Nothing good. Prepare as you are able."

> Go home and quietly prepare extra food, a blade near the door

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.
