# Livia the Scholar

**Autumn, Day 26, Year 866**

The wolves have been seen again. The Ashwood is not what anyone thought it was.

Oswin runs into town at first light, sheep pen empty behind them. "Three of them," they gasp. "Big ones, grey. They came out of the Ashwood." The flock is scattered across the hillside. Hilda is already checking their bow outside the tavern. "I have been waiting for this," the hunter says quietly. "They have been circling closer for a month. Something in the wood has pushed them out." They look at you. "You coming?"

> Warn the other shepherds and help them secure their pens first

You spend the morning going door to door. Most are grateful. Two ignore you. By afternoon the pens are tighter and people are keeping their dogs close. Oswin finds you at day's end. "My flock is accounted for. Every one." Then they drop their voice. "Marren's boy was out in the north pasture this morning. He saw the wolves pass. He says there were not three. He says there were six. And something with them..." they hesitate. "He does not have words for it. He is eleven. He said it walked upright."

> Find Hilda and tell them what you heard

The boy is steady for eleven, not prone to invention. He describes it clearly: taller than a man, moving at the edge of the wolf pack, not chasing them and not fleeing with them. Accompanying them. He watched until it turned and looked directly at him from too far away, he says, to have seen his face. Then it turned back and continued north, and the wolves went with it. He went home. He did not tell his father. "It did not feel like a monster," the boy says. "It felt like it was going somewhere."

> Report this to the guard and Hilda, wait for a larger party

You come back out of the Ashwood with dusk at your back and something changed in your chest, not quite fear, not quite wonder, a third thing between them. Oswin is at the edge of the pasture, waiting. Hilda is somewhere in the village and will want to know what you found. What you tell them, and how much of it, will shape what Ironhaven does next about the Ashwood.

> Tell Oswin and Hilda everything, all of it

Whatever you shared or kept about the Ashwood, the town has a wolf story now. Over the next week, it moves through the lanes the way all stories do, losing specifics and gaining drama. By the time it reaches the market day conversation, the thin fleeing wolves have become enormous hunting animals, and the warm stone in the clearing has become either nothing at all or something sinister, depending on who is telling it. You hear your own experience reflected back to you in shapes you do not recognize.

> Correct the story when you hear it wrong. The truth matters here.

Three weeks after the sighting, the sheep pens are reinforced and the north pasture boundary has a new fence line that the farmers paid for themselves without being asked. The Ashwood is the same. The wolves are somewhere in it. The stone in the clearing is still warm, or warm enough. The question the wolf sighting raised is not answered. It has been set aside with practical measures. That may be enough. Or it may be the kind of setting-aside that returns on its own schedule.
