# Livia the Scholar

**Winter, Day 20, Year 875**

The guild dispute has drawn in a guard who is taking coin from both sides. Someone needs to deal with it.

Aldric stops you near the workshop lane, one hand resting on their belt. "Busy time, isn't it. All this guild trouble." They lower their voice. "Accidents happen to folk who don't have friends in the guard. Call it a protection fee. Ten silver and I make sure your door stays quiet." The lane is empty except for Lydia crossing at the far end, who may or may not have heard.

> Refuse flatly and walk away

Aldric watches you go with flat eyes. "Your choice." That evening, you notice a scratch on your door lock and a broken shutter latch. Nothing stolen, just a message. You sleep lightly. The next morning Aldric passes you in the lane without speaking, which is its own kind of message. This is not over unless someone makes it over.

> Confront Aldric again, this time with names of witnesses

Aldric's face cycles through three expressions quickly: contempt, calculation, and then something like wariness. They did not expect this. "You have nothing that would hold in front of the captain," they say, but the confidence is constructed. They are not sure of it. "Those people would not testify." They are trying to work out how much you actually have.

> Demand they return what they took from the others, in exchange for silence

The situation is not resolved. It is changed. Aldric is either paid, warned, reported, or confronted, but the guild dispute that enabled them still simmers. The captain knows more than they did yesterday. The guilds are watching to see how this shakes out. Your position in the middle of it has been established whether you intended it or not. What happens at the guild vote will tell everyone what the town's institutions are actually worth.

> Stay involved. This connects to the guild vote.

There is still daylight. The story is not yet finished.

> Stall. Say you do not carry that much coin. Ask if you can bring it tomorrow.

Aldric studies you, calculating whether you are about to run or genuinely short of silver. "Tomorrow morning," they say finally. "Before the market opens." They walk away without looking back, the easy confidence of someone who is used to being paid. You have until morning. That is enough time to do several things.

> Decide to just pay in the morning and end the problem cheaply

Aldric palms the coins without blinking. "Smart choice. You have a good day now." They stroll away, unhurried. You feel the lightness of ten silver missing from your purse and something heavier in its place. It is not guilt exactly, it is the weight of knowing this will happen again. Perhaps to you. Perhaps to someone else. The choice to pay does not end the problem, it only relocates it.

> Change your mind. Find Brenna.

Aldric goes pale. They say nothing as you turn toward the guardhouse. Brenna listens without interrupting, face unreadable. "Leave it with me," they say at last. "And thank you for your courage." They do not use the word courage lightly. You notice that the door is closed, the latch is firm, and the captain does not reach for a ledger immediately. They are thinking first.

> Add that the shakedown is tied to the guild dispute, if you believe it is

Brenna sets down their quill and folds their hands. They have been listening with the particular attention of someone who already suspected something was wrong and is now hearing their suspicion confirmed in detail. "This is not the first complaint of this kind," they say, carefully. "But you are the first to bring it with evidence." They look at you. "I am going to open an inquiry. Aldric will know it came from you. Do you understand what you are agreeing to?"

> Say yes. You understand. Proceed.

Brenna writes two lines and seals them. An inquiry is now a formal thing. Aldric will be confined to light duties while it runs. The inquiry will take days and the outcome is not guaranteed, but the mechanism has been set in motion. As you leave the guardhouse you pass Aldric in the lane. They do not speak. They watch you with the still, flat attention of someone who is marking a record.

> Stay visible and calm. The inquiry is your protection now.

The situation is not resolved. It is changed. Aldric is either paid, warned, reported, or confronted, but the guild dispute that enabled them still simmers. The captain knows more than they did yesterday. The guilds are watching to see how this shakes out. Your position in the middle of it has been established whether you intended it or not. What happens at the guild vote will tell everyone what the town's institutions are actually worth.

> Stay involved. This connects to the guild vote.

The day draws to a close.
